Deep Life Stack v2.0

Framework ▼

Two stages. The first builds you into a capable human being. The second cultivates a life of depth and meaning. Dedicate 1–3 weeks to each layer, then revisit the full stack every 6–12 months.

Stage 1 — Become a Capable Human Being
1

Discipline

Change your self-identity to someone who can persist with difficult things. Establish keystone habits:

  • Body — Daily physical practice
  • Mind — Daily cognitive sharpening
  • Heart — Daily connection
2

Control

Gain control over your time and obligations. Implement multi-scale planning. Build a capture system. Tame email and obligations.

3

Craft / Skill Development

Deliberately improve skills around valuable areas. Pursue deliberate practice in your primary craft.

4

Simplification

Simplify your technological interactions. Implement the phone foyer method. Reduce digital noise.

Stage 2 — Cultivate Depth
5

Values & Code

Define a personal code that guides decisions and actions. Establish rituals that connect you to your values.

6

Service & Community

Use your capabilities to serve others. Build strong relationships through sacrificing time and attention.

7

Vision & Transformation

This is where lifestyle-centric planning lives. With all lower layers solid, plan for the remarkable.

Productivity Funnel

Capture - Configure - Control ▼

1. Capture

Get everything out of your head. Use a WorkingMemory.txt as temporary storage.

  • Master to-do list on your computer
  • Master calendar for all commitments
  • WorkingMemory.txt for in-the-moment capture
  • Collection column in daily time-block page

2. Configure

Organize captured items into structured systems. Use role-based task boards.

  • Role-based task boards (Trello, Notion, etc.)
  • Each task has context and next actions
  • Autopilot schedule for recurring tasks
  • Project-level planning documents

3. Control

Multi-scale planning is the control layer. Pull tasks into quarterly, weekly, daily plans.

  • Quarterly plan sets direction
  • Weekly plan translates to action
  • Daily time-blocking executes
  • Shutdown ritual closes the loop

Shutdown Ritual

End of Day ▼

"When you're done, you're done. A shutdown ritual helps your mind shift completely from work mode to non-work mode."

— Cal Newport

Daily Shutdown Checklist

Run through this 3–5 minute ritual at the end of every workday.

  1. Process collection column — Review every item captured during the day.
  2. Check email inbox — Process or schedule responses.
  3. Review task boards — Scan active projects. Confirm nothing urgent is forgotten.
  4. Review calendar — Check tomorrow and next 2–3 days.
  5. Update weekly plan — Adjust if today shifted priorities.
  6. Set tomorrow's rough plan — Draft a preliminary time-block schedule.
  7. Close all work tabs and apps — Physically separate from work tools.
